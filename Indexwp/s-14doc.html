What Is A Remix?

What does it mean when you make art, a song or a film; do you own it? What happens when someone changes it, and makes something new? Many people claim that making these changes, or remixing as it were, is stealing a person’s intellectual property. Others, however, claim that everything is a remix and in order to create, we must reuse. This assignment will discuss when a remix is appropriate and if it can be used for monetary gain.

To begin, what is a remix? When someone takes another’s work of art and adds to it or changes it into something nearly unrecognizable, it becomes a remix. A remix is a person’s way of spicing up something they loved with their own artistic flair. For others, it is a way to build on an already great idea. As Henry Ford once said, “I invented nothing new. I simply assembled the discoveries of other men”. This holds true for remixes as well; remixing builds upon the ideas of others which will later be even better. Remixing can be another art form of creativity, where the artist makes something even better. It can also reach out to other audiences. For example, if someone made a dubstep version of a country song, there would be two audiences rather than one. Unfortunately, some people refer to a remix as plagiarism.

According to current american copyright law, creating and selling or showing a remix as your own is illegal. The copyright laws that exist were made to encourage invention and creation. For many, the incentive of keeping their ideas and creations as their own was enticing. Patents and other documentation were made to protect the creator’s ideas from being copied. Today however, has resulted in a slowing of new ideas. Inventors and mega corporations hide their ideas under lock and key, away from people who want to improve them. So, should it be illegal to make and show off a remix or other creation? Whose property is it? My personal opinion would say that it is ok to remix as long as it is for the sake of improvement, art and creativity. However I’m not sure whether or not selling it would be allowed. It depends on how much work you put into it compared to the original creator, and how similar it is/isn’t.
